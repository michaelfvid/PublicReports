Key Maintenance Challenges Identified

Analysis of maintenance data reveals five critical challenges that significantly impact operational reliability and maintenance costs on the FPSO Cidade de Ilhabela:

1. Recurring Water Injection System Failures

The water injection system, particularly related to SRP membrane fouling and cleaning, has been responsible for multiple operational stoppages over the analyzed period. These failures have resulted in over $4.4 million in charter revenue losses and represent a persistent issue despite regular maintenance.

2. High Gas Turbine Maintenance Costs

Gas turbine healthcare contracts represent nearly 21% of the total maintenance budget ($4.96M annually), with additional costs for L1, L2, and L3 maintenance activities. Current maintenance approach appears to be primarily time-based rather than condition-based.

3. Reactive Approach to Equipment Failures

Historical failure data suggests a predominantly reactive maintenance approach, with equipment being run until failure or maintained on fixed schedules rather than based on actual condition. This is particularly evident in the recurring nature of similar failures.

4. Suboptimal Inspection Planning

Significant budget allocation for various inspection activities (over $3M annually) suggests potential inefficiencies in inspection planning and execution. Many inspections appear to be calendar-based rather than risk or condition-based.

5. Limited Equipment Health Visibility

The recurrence of similar failures over extended periods indicates limited visibility into developing equipment issues. Current monitoring systems do not appear to provide adequate advance warning of impending failures.
